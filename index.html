📜 General Rules
🌐
GlobalPath International Travel Standards
Covering all major intercontinental routes
  • All travelers must possess a valid international passport with a minimum of 6 months validity beyond the intended stay.
  • Visa requirements vary by nationality and destination. Applicants must verify visa eligibility prior to booking travel.
  • GlobalPath Immigration Services facilitates applications but does not guarantee visa approval — final decisions rest with immigration authorities.
  • Submission of false, altered, or fraudulent documents will result in immediate rejection and may lead to legal consequences.
  • All documents submitted must be original or certified copies. Photocopies without notarization are not accepted.
  • Processing times vary: standard 10–15 business days; express 5–7 business days (additional fees apply).
  • Applicants must maintain health insurance valid in their destination country for the duration of travel.
  • GlobalPath reserves the right to request additional documentation at any stage of the process.
🌏
Asia
Dubai (UAE) · Japan · Singapore · India · China · Thailand
  • Dubai (UAE): Visa on arrival available for many nationalities for stays up to 30 days. Tourist visa extensions available in-country. No visa required for GCC residents.
  • Japan: Visa-free entry for up to 90 days for many nationalities. Work and student visas require sponsorship and prior approval.
  • Singapore: Visa-free for most Commonwealth and EU nationals for 30–90 days. All travelers must show proof of onward/return travel.
  • India: e-Visa available online for most nationalities. Medical and employment visas require specific supporting letters.
  • Required Documents for Asia travel: Valid passport, return flight ticket, hotel booking confirmation, proof of funds (minimum $1,000 USD equivalent), travel insurance, and guarantor ID where required.
  • Transitional Movements: Transit visas required for layovers exceeding 24 hours in UAE, China (without TWOV eligibility), and India.
  • Prohibited Items: Strict drug laws apply across all Asian destinations. UAE has zero tolerance for controlled substances. Penalties include life imprisonment or deportation.
  • Cultural Rules: Dress modestly in Dubai and other Islamic destinations. Religious sites require specific dress codes throughout Asia.
🌍
Africa
South Africa · Morocco · Nigeria · Kenya · Egypt · Ghana
  • South Africa: Visa-free for most EU, UK, and US nationals for up to 90 days. Yellow fever vaccination required if arriving from endemic countries.
  • Morocco: Visa-free for 90 days for most Western passports. Travelers must have accommodation bookings confirmed prior to entry.
  • Kenya: Single-entry East Africa Tourist Visa (shared with Uganda and Rwanda) available. e-Visa mandatory for most nationalities.
  • Egypt: Visa on arrival available for many nationalities ($25 USD fee). Sinai-only permit is cheaper but restricts movement to Red Sea coastal areas.
  • Required Documents: Valid passport (6+ months validity), proof of funds, vaccination certificate (Yellow Fever, COVID-19 where required), travel insurance, return ticket.
  • Transitional Movements: Intra-Africa travel may require additional transit visas. Nigerian airports do not offer transit visas — connecting passengers must hold full Nigerian visas.
  • Health Requirements: Yellow fever vaccination card is mandatory when entering many African nations from endemic regions. Malaria prophylaxis strongly recommended.
  • Currency: Foreign exchange must be declared upon entry in South Africa if carrying ZAR 25,000 or more or equivalent.
🌎
North America
United States · Canada · Mexico · Caribbean
  • United States: ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization) required for Visa Waiver Program countries. All other nationalities must obtain a B-1/B-2 tourist visa through US Embassy.
  • Canada: eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) required for visa-exempt nationals traveling by air. Biometrics required for most non-exempt applicants.
  • Mexico: Visa-free entry for most nationalities. FMM tourist card required on arrival (completed on arrival or online).
  • Required Documents: Valid passport, visa/ESTA/eTA, proof of accommodation, return tickets, bank statements (3 months minimum), travel insurance covering $100,000+ medical.
  • US Border Entry: CBSA and CBP officers have broad authority to question and search. All electronic devices may be searched at port of entry.
  • Transitional Movements: Transit through the US requires a valid US visa or ESTA even for layovers, unless connecting airside without clearing customs.
  • Canada–US Border: Crossing by land requires NEXUS card or full visa documentation. Pre-clearance available at major Canadian airports for US-bound flights.
🌎
South America
Brazil · Argentina · Colombia · Peru · Chile
  • Brazil: Visa-free for most Western passports. US, Canada, and Australian citizens now enjoy visa-free access. Yellow fever vaccination required for Amazon region visits.
  • Argentina: Visa-free for most nationalities up to 90 days. Citizens of some nations pay a reciprocity fee online before travel.
  • Colombia: Visa-free for most nationalities for 90 days (extendable to 180 days per year). Onward ticket required at entry.
  • Peru: Visa-free for most nationalities. Altitude sickness preparation advisable for Machu Picchu and Andean destinations (3,400m+).
  • Required Documents: Valid passport, onward/return ticket, proof of sufficient funds, travel insurance, accommodation confirmation.
  • Transitional Movements: South American nations share Mercosur agreements allowing ID-only travel between member states for citizens. Foreign nationals still require full passport documentation.
  • Health Advisory: Yellow fever, typhoid, and hepatitis A vaccinations strongly recommended. Some Brazilian regions have Zika virus risk — pregnant travelers should consult physicians.
🏛
Europe
Schengen Area · UK · Switzerland · Norway · Iceland
  • Schengen Area (26 countries): Single Schengen visa grants access to all member states for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. First point of entry must be the main destination country's embassy visa.
  • United Kingdom: No longer in Schengen. Separate UK Standard Visitor Visa required for non-exempt nationals. ETA (Electronic Travel Authorisation) now required for visa-exempt nationals arriving by air.
  • ETIAS (Coming Soon): EU ETIAS travel authorization will be required for all non-EU/EEA visa-exempt nationals from 2025 onward. Apply online prior to departure.
  • Required Documents: Passport (valid 3+ months beyond intended stay), visa/ETIAS, travel insurance (minimum €30,000 medical coverage), round-trip tickets, proof of accommodation and funds.
  • Transitional Movements: Internal Schengen borders are open. Non-EU nationals are subject to random checks. Keep travel documents accessible at all times within the Schengen zone.
  • Customs: Strict limits on alcohol, tobacco, and cash (€10,000+) declarations required when entering the Schengen zone from outside.
  • Student / Work Visas: Separate long-stay national visas (D-visas) required for stays exceeding 90 days. These must be applied at individual country embassies.
🦘
Australia & Oceania
Australia · New Zealand · Fiji · Papua New Guinea
  • Australia: All non-citizen visitors must hold a valid Australian visa. The eVisitor (subclass 651) and ETA (subclass 601) are available to eligible passport holders online.
  • Tourist Visa (subclass 600): Required for nationalities not eligible for eVisitor or ETA. Allows stays of up to 3, 6, or 12 months depending on approval.
  • New Zealand: NZeTA (Electronic Travel Authority) required for visa-waiver countries. International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy (IVL) applies — NZD $35 per entry.
  • Required Documents: Valid passport (6+ months), valid Australian/NZ visa or ETA, return air ticket, proof of accommodation, travel insurance, sufficient funds proof.
  • Biosecurity — Critical: Australia has strict biosecurity laws. Declare all food, plant material, and animal products. Failure to declare attracts fines of AUD $420+ on the spot or criminal prosecution.
  • Transitional Movements: Trans-Tasman Travel Arrangement allows free movement between Australia and New Zealand for citizens only. All others require separate visas for each country.
  • Health: No mandatory vaccination requirements for most travelers. COVID-related rules may still apply — check Department of Home Affairs website before travel.

Required Documents (All Routes)
  • Valid international passport (min. 6 months beyond travel dates)
  • Applicable visa or electronic authorization
  • Return or onward travel ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host letter)
  • Travel health insurance (minimum $50,000 USD coverage)
  • Proof of sufficient funds (bank statements, 3 months)

Border & Customs Rules
  • Declare all cash equivalents above equivalent of $10,000 USD
  • Prohibited items vary by country — research destination-specific restrictions
  • Electronic devices may be searched without warrant at border entry points
  • Overstaying your visa has serious long-term consequences including bans

Legal Obligations

  • Respect religious sites, dress codes, and cultural norms
  • Do not engage in unauthorized employment on tourist visas
  • Register with your home country's embassy in case of emergencies

Health & Safety

  • Carry prescription medication with official doctor's letter and documentation
  • Register emergency contacts with GlobalPath before departure
  • Purchase comprehensive travel insurance covering medical evacuation
